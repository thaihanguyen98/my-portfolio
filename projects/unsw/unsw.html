UNSW Short Course Website Redesign

Website design and Information Architecture focus for short course

I was assigned a project for the UNSW Short course to re-design a website that is accessible and easy for users who are looking for a course. We had two weeks to research, design, prototype and test a website for them.

Project type:

Academy XI

My role

UX/ UI designer

Deliverables

Research and analysis, Usability Testings, Card Sorting, Information Architecture, content strategy, personas, wire-frame, prototypes, UI Design and presentation.

Tools

Miro, Zoom and Figma

Duration

2 weeks (March 2020)

Project goals

  • Redesign the current website to be an intuitive website and improve the user experience for the students who will be using the website.
  • Rebuild information architecture by reflecting real students' use cases and up-to-date content to register for social events, check their timetable, engage with the student online and much more.
  • Delivery of low and high-fidelity UX UI design for the development process.

Research discoveries

User surveys

We conducted interviews with 7 participants that involve card sorting, usability testing on the current website and understanding their thoughts, feelings and preferences when looking for a course. We find that:

Key findings and insights

  • The website is not user-friendly and accessible. There are no results after typing in the search box.
  • Content is disorganised and overwhelming.
  • Unclear navigation and information architecture.
  • Reason for a career change: unfulfilled job; unhappiness and looking for an upskill.Research the course through Google, Linkedin and recommendation from peers.
  • Limitation #1: Commitment and afraid that they will make the wrong decisions.
  • Limitation #2: Course price can be expensive.
